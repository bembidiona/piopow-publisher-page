About Piopow:
  • Explosive turn-based roguelite.
  • Destructible terrain.
  • Levels are procedurally generated, so every playthrough is different.
  • A culinary story told with ingame cutscenes, and a bunch of surprises.
  • First campaign is 20 levels divided over 4 different areas, which unlocks the 2nd character's quest.
  • Second campaign is hard, and unlocks the 5th area and the bombastic true ending.
  • It's super polished and cute. Aims to be a new little gem in the NES library.
  • The game teaches itself. It was heavily playtested on people with zero know-how of the rogue genre.
  • Accessibility option to disable color flashes.
Tech & Numbers
  • It uses an NROM board like Micro Mages or Nebs 'n Debs.
  • 2 playable characters.
  • 6 collectible items.
  • 7 music tracks.
  • 12 visually different enemies (and not using palette swaps).
  • Supports NTSC and PAL.

About Me:
  • I'm from Argentina, and I will be moving to Tierra del Fuego soon. So, maybe this will be the most southern 8-bit game ever ?
  • I feel culturally attached to the Famicom more than the NES, so the possibility of making Famicom releases is a big plus for me! (The only market when I was a child was: US roms, in Famicom cartridges, being played on PAL TVs. A total mess ha, but those little colorful cases stayed forever in my heart).
